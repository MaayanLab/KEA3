About BINDer


BINDer (The Bio Interaction Networks Directory) is a platform to store network files related to kinase-substrate, protein-protein, and transcription factor-target interactions. Data was mined from over 18 interaction databases and processed into GMT and SIG files, and these files are located in the tab corresponding to the interaction type of interest.

BINDer was created as part of the KEA3 project, which seeks to update the KEA2 (Kinase Enrichment Analysis 2) tool built in 2009. KEA2 takes an input of a gene list and outputs a list of associated kinases, but many kinase-substrate databases that were originally included in KEA2 have expanded and new kinase-substrate databases have emerged. Thus, the purpose of the KEA3 project is to collect and process new data pertaining to kinase-substrate interactions and expand the KEA2 database, ultimately allowing for a more comprehensive understanding of the human kinome.

For further information about using the site, please refer to the "Help" tab above. To contribute more resources to the website, please visit the "Contribute" tab above.
